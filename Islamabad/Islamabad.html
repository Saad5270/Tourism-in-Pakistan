Islamabad

Islamabad has a reputation of being a thriving, business-minded capital that lies in the centre of Pakistan's growing urbanized scene. The white marble Faisal Mosque, which is an important landmark of the city one of the largest mosques in the world, is a central point of tourists as is the bustling Jinnah Market.The capital city of Pakistan, Islamabad is located in the northwest of the country on Potohar Plateau. This area has been significant in history for being a part of the crossroads of the Rawalpindi and the North West Frontier Province. The city was built in 1960 to replace Karachi as the Pakistani capital, which it has been since 1963. Due to Islamabad's proximity to Rawalpindi, they are considered sister cities. Compared to other cities of the country, Islamabad is a clean, spacious and quiet city with lots of greeneries. The site of the city has a history going back to the earliest human habitations in Asia. This area has seen the first settlement of Aryans from Central Asia, ancient caravans passing from Central Asia, and the massive armies of Tamerlane and Alexander. To the north of the city you will find the Margalla Hills. Hot summers, monsoon rains and cold winters with sparse snowfall in the hills almost summarize the climate of this area. Islamabad also has a rich wildlife ranging from wild boars to leopards.

Shah Faisal Mosque

If you're looking for a lovely site to visit in Islamabad at night, this should be at the top of your list. Because it is so magnificent and stunning, anyone who observes it may fall in love with its beauty. The tented mosque is stretched over a surface area of 5000 square meters. Also, it is acknowledged as one of Islamabad's most famous landmarks in trip photography.

Saidpur Village

Saidpur is a terrific destination to explore the true village life in this era of technology. If you're looking for a break from the monotonous routine of your city's glittering malls and crowded bazaars, this is the right place to go. This is one of Islamabad's most picturesque locations. It is great for individuals who have never visited a village in their lives. You can also visit a Hindu temple and a Sikh Gurdwara while roaming around the square-roofed village in the heart of the Margalla hills.

Shah Allah Ditta Caves

One of the best locations to see in Islamabad is the Allah Ditta Caves. It is a historical site in the Margalla foothills that people should not miss. The settlement near the caves is claimed to be 700 years old. It once served as a link between Kabul and Gandharan City. The caves are roughly about 2400 years old, according to experts. The caves are supposed to be Buddhist relics, Aurangzeb period relics, and Hindu period relics.

Art and Craft Village

The Art and Craft Village is open until late at night. It is an amazing spot to visit in Islamabad if you are a tourist who enjoys cultural artifacts and traditional art and crafts. Every form of traditional, cultural antique artifact about Pakistan that you won't find in any marketplace or store will be found here. It covers facts about Pakistan's culture, including our journey and the country's fundamental roots. You can also purchase vintage models of cultural art crafts to use as decorative items in your home.
